Characters

In Godhood there are four characters available to play.

The Fighter

The fighter embodies the common mans perception of what being a hero is all about. He's big, he's tough, he's a hit with the ladies, and has a one-liner for every situation. When he's not brutalizing his enemies, the fighter is something of a people-person, and can enlist civilians through a combination of rabble rousing and good, old fashioned, military-style conscription.

The Magician

After graduating from the Academy of Magic the magician became interested in the Eye of Ascension, some say to the point of obsession. Now it has been discovered and he must be the first to have it, it would be misused in any other fools hands.

The magician is the only player that can read any spellbooks that you happen to find lying around.

The Cleric

Drawn to Stutville by rumours of the Ascension, the cleric is a holy man of unequaled piety. In the interests of the good of the world, not to mention his own gods personal agenda, the resurection of Baromophet must be stopped before it starts.

Due to the importance of his quest, the cleric can request aid from his god through prayer.

The Thief

The thief had an epiphany at a very young age, which, if she had ever bothered to put it into words, would have read something like

"obtaining the maximum rewards for exerting the least effort, is very different to exerting the least effort for the maximum rewards".

Although she keeps her profession a secret, the thief puts people she meets on edge, maybe it's the wolfish grin, or the fact that she's picking your pocket, but people tend to avoid her, and she prefers it that way anyway.

The thief has been known to dispatch her enemies with a single, fatal, blow.

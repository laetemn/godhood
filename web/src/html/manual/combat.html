Combat

Depending on your style of play, you may find yourself engaged in combat more than once during the game. If so, this handy guide will teach you the basics, so you won't completely disgrace yourself.

Attacking

You can attack just about anything in the game. If someone or something is in your way, odds are a few chops from your broad sword will fix the problem. You mightn't win any popularity contests for doing this, but expediency often trumps virtue in this day and age.

To attack an object you will need to select an attack from your action menu. Some items give you additional attack options, however most characters will have an intrinsic attack of some description. Have a look for an action with a "†" symbol after it.

After you select this action you will enter Attack mode. A target will appear on the screen, this will be sitting on the closest enemy who is in range. You can simply press Fire or Game A to attack this monster. If you wish to attack another square you can move the target to that square using the direction keys on your keypad.

While in Attack mode, the following keys perform the following actions
Up Move the target in a north-easterly direction
Down Move the target in a south-westerly direction
Left Move the target in a north-westerly direction
Right Move the target in a south-easterly direction
Fire or Game A Perform the attack now
Game B Toggle information on the targeted monster(s)
Game C Go to the next enemy in range

You can cancel your attack at any point by simply selecting another action to perform.

Default Attacks
If all this sounds a bit hard, don't worry, most of the time you can use your characters default attack. You do this by simply walking into the monster you wish to attack while in Move mode. Your character will automatically select what he/she thinks is the best attack for the job.

Being Attacked

Sometimes you will meet a particularly beligerent individual who means to do you harm. If this does happen, it is very likely that you will find yourself under attack. In Godhood your armour will reduce the chance that you are hit, however the attackers level of experience will increase the chance that they will hit you. If you are struck, you will stop what ever you are doing (casting a spell, defending, sleeping, etc...) and a drop of blood will appear above your head, as well as a bar indicating your health.

Death and Other Inconveniences

If a monster is attacked often enough he or she will eventually die. Indeed this is generally the desired result of any attack. Some attacks will, in addition to injuring the victim, cause the victim additional discomfort. For example, a Flaming Sword, will, in addition to the damage inflicted by the sword, set the target on fire!

If your character dies then the game ends.

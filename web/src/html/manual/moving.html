Getting Around

In Godhood you may find that you need to move from one location to another. If you do find yourself in this situation, don't panic, this section will guide you through the basics of perambulation.

Turns

Godhood is a turn-based game, this means that you can't do anything (except watch helplessly) until it is your turn. The monster who is currently having a turn will have its name displayed in the bottom left corner, and will be standing on top of an octogram1. When it is your turn, you will notice a health bar, an icon indicating the mode that your character is in, and a number indicating the number of turn units (TUs) available to your character in the bottom left corner.

Every action in the game uses up a certain number of TUs, this number appears in brackets at the end of the actions description. If there are not enough TUs to perform an action in this turn, you'll either have to do something else, or wait until your next turn by selecting End Turn.

Usually your character will start his/her turn in Move Mode, or in other words, ready to walk around.

Walking

Walking is, by far, the most common method of transport in the realm. To walk around simply select Move from the menu then press a direction on your phones keypad2.

You may notice that you can only go in four directions, but there are 8 tiles adjacent to your character. To access the other four tiles press the Fire or Game A key on your phone, you'll notice that the arrows in the bottom right-hand corner have rotated by 45 degrees.


While in Move mode, the following keys perform the following actions
Up Move either north or north-east
Down Move either south or south-west
Left Move either west or north-west direction
Right Move either east or south-east direction
Fire or Game A Switch between north, south, east, west navigation and north-east, south-east, north-west, south-west navigation.
Game B Pick up what ever is below you

Flying

If you do happen to be a creature with the gift of flight, movement is still very similar to walking, however you can also effortlessly traverse chasms as well.

Switching Places

Switching places with another monster is very handy for a lot of reasons. Firstly it allows you to move a monster without it having to be that monsters turn, secondly the monster that is swapping in will not be immediately attacked by any enemies in the vicinity, as it would have been if it was moving normally.

You may exchange places with monsters as long as they meet the following criteria

  • the monster swapping in has enough points to move,
  • the monster swapping out has enough points to move, and
  • the monster swapping out is on the same side, or is friendly with, the monster swapping in

Doors

In Godhood all doors are locked. It is left as an exercise for the reader to figure out how to deal with them.

Changing Rooms

Godhood has several rooms for you to explore. To move between them you'll need to locate portal of some variety. Examples of portals include, doorways, trapdoors and ladders. Portals can be identified by the downward facing arrow above them, this indicates that you can enter. To use a portal, you'll need to stand directly ontop of it, and select the "Enter" option.

1. Octogram: A star with eight points

2. You may need to consult your phones manual if you are unsure how to do this.
